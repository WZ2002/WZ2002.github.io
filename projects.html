01 Projects & leadership

Building, not just analyzing

  1. 01 Co-founder · 2025–present

    ONE-AGI

    A cross-campus AI organization spanning Johns Hopkins and UC Berkeley (partnered with the Berkeley Emerging Technology Association), grown to ~200 members. I run hands-on workshops on LLM agents, AIGC, and AI-agent prototyping, and lead discussions on AI safety, algorithmic fairness, and the societal impacts of AGI.

    • ~200 members
    • 8 events
    • ~50 / event
    • JHU × UC Berkeley
  2. 02 Cato Institute · 2026

    Bring-Your-Own-Power policy database

    A 50-state, 10-year Bring-Your-Own-Power (BYOP) database spanning both policy and projects: it catalogues related state-level legislation by status and mechanism, and benchmarks every operational BYOP energy project of the past decade (cross-verified across project reports, certifications, official sites, and news) for generating capacity and underlying technology. It supplied the data and case evidence behind a Cato senior scholar's Congressional testimony and briefings with legislators.

    • 50 states
    • 10-year span
    • Multi-source verified
    • Congressional testimony
  3. 03 ALEC · 2025

    50-state legislative survey, K-12 device policy

    A 50-state survey (27 states, 29 measures) of K-12 classroom cell-phone policy, with bill-level sourcing and a data visualization, supporting model-policy work on student device use in schools.

    • 27 states
    • 29 measures
    • Data visualization
  4. 04 Designer & coordinator · 2023

    Museum Decolonization & Inclusivity Project

    Designed accessibility strategies to make Australian Museum exhibits navigable for non-English-speaking visitors, working across disciplines through field research and stakeholder presentations. The work drew praise from museum staff and earned the highest grade in the cohort.

    • Australian Museum
    • Accessibility design
    • Highest grade in class

02 Community & service

Beyond the work

Long-term community service and support for people in difficult circumstances has been a lasting commitment of mine: teaching children, supporting communities in need, and leading student advocacy across China, Thailand, and Australia, much of it still ongoing.

Teaching & education

  • Mental-health education module

    Development Society · Group Leader · 2021–2022

    Led a 5-person team to bring mental-health education to elementary students in rural Hunan and Chongqing, building an animated teaching module end to end (animation, voiceover, and script) and reaching 700+ students. The program earned commendations from local schools and education authorities.

  • Shaanxi Aiding Center for Children in Difficult Circumstances

    Volunteer Teacher · Shaanxi, China · Since 2014

    A decade of work with the children of incarcerated parents, beginning as a teaching assistant and serving as lead teacher since 2017. Teaches Chinese classical studies, supports early-childhood learning, runs fundraising drives, and has personally helped fund two peers' college education.

  • Northern Thailand Volunteer Program

    Volunteer Teacher & Group Leader · Pai Dang Village, Thailand · 2016–2017

    Led a 6-person team teaching the descendants of the Chinese Expeditionary Force, from calligraphy and lion dance to classical studies, and helped organize improvements to the village school.

Community service

  • Yellow River Soup Kitchen

    Volunteer Worker · Xi'an, China · Since 2018

    Serves people experiencing homelessness, distributing food, clothing, and hygiene supplies twice a week at a local church and through monthly street outreach, and keeps the donation warehouse running, adapting operations through the pandemic.

  • Clayton Wesley Uniting Church

    Volunteer Worker · Adelaide, Australia · 2018–2019

    Faith-based service supporting refugees: providing emotional support through resettlement and helping organize donation drives and charity sales.

Leadership & advocacy

  • International Students' Association

    President · Seymour College, Adelaide · 2018–2019

    Led the International Students' Association at Seymour College, representing international students and building community across the school.

  • Anti-discrimination & Indigenous rights group

    Group Leader · Seymour College, Adelaide · 2019

    Led peer advocacy on anti-discrimination and Indigenous rights, delivering school assemblies and presentations and facilitating small-group discussions.
